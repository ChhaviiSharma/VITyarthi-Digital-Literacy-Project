CASE STUDY 

TOPIC : Cyberbullying 

Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where a person is repeatedly targeted using digital platforms such as social media, messaging apps, or online forums. A realistic example involves a 16-year-old student, Riya, who posted a class presentation video on her school group. Initially, a few classmates began mocking her accent in comments. Soon, screenshots were shared in other groups, and edited memes were created to embarrass her.

The incident typically unfolds step-by-step. First, a harmless post becomes the trigger. Second, a few individuals post negative comments or jokes. Third, the content spreads as others share, like, and add more hurtful messages. Fourth, fake accounts may be created to continue harassment anonymously. Finally, the victim is repeatedly tagged or messaged, making it difficult to escape the situation.

Students and teenagers are usually targeted because they are highly active online and often sensitive to peer opinion. Victims may feel anxious, embarrassed, and isolated. In Riya’s case, she stopped participating in class discussions and avoided school activities. Her academic performance declined, and she experienced stress and low self-confidence.

The consequences of cyberbullying can be serious. Emotional distress, depression, and social withdrawal are common. In extreme cases, it may lead to long-term mental health issues. For the bullies, there can also be disciplinary action from school authorities and possible legal consequences under cyber laws.
This case highlights the importance of respectful online behavior, reporting abusive content, and supporting victims. Early intervention by teachers, parents, and peers can help stop cyberbullying and create a safer digital environment.
